Present Perfect vs Past Simple — Which One?

B1 — grammar, vocabulary, listening, reading, writing

Kenji: Have you ever eaten at that new restaurant?

Sofía: Yes! I have been there twice. I went on Friday... and I went back on Saturday. No regrets.

Ben: I have never tried it... because I lost my wallet last week.

Abuela Carmen: I have cooked ten thousand dinners in my life, and nobody ever paid me. Invite her, Ben.

Common mistake: “I have visited Paris last year.” — Correct: “I visited Paris last year.”

Choose past simple when the moment is closed (yesterday, in 2020, ago); choose present perfect when the time is still open or the exact moment doesn't matter.

Which one? Follow the time signal
SignalTenseExample
Finished moment: yesterday, last year, in 2020, agoPAST SIMPLEI went to Madrid in 2020
Open time / whole life: ever, never, already, yet, just, for, sincePRESENT PERFECTI have been to Madrid twice
The interview ruleperfect opens, past gives the detailsHave you ever been...? Yes — I went in 2020
The news ruleperfect announces, past explainsBen has broken his arm! He fell off a hammock
Same fact, two frameslife vs momentI have seen that movie (life) · I saw it last night (moment)
  • past yesterday — ayer
  • past last year — el año pasado
  • past two days ago — hace dos días
  • past in 2020 — en 2020
  • past when I was a child — cuando era niño/a
  • past last night — anoche
  • perfect ever / never — alguna vez / nunca
  • perfect already / yet — ya / todavía
  • perfect just — recién / hace un momento
  • perfect for / since — durante / desde
  • perfect twice / three times — dos / tres veces (en la vida)
  • perfect this week — esta semana (aún abierta)
  • I have been to Madrid twice. I went there in 2020 and 2023.
  • Ben has broken his arm three times in his life.
  • Ben broke his arm last year.
  • Sofía has already sent the email. She sent it this morning.
  • I haven't seen that movie yet.
  • I didn't see the movie last night.
  • Kenji hasn't visited Cartagena yet, but he visited Bogotá in May.
  • Have you ever been to Madrid?
  • When did you go there?
  • Has she sent the email yet?
  • Have you finished? Yes, I have — I finished an hour ago.
  • Did you go out last night? No, I didn't.
  • Has Ben ever broken a bone? Yes, he has.

Practice

  1. I ___ Paris last year. (visited, have visited, have visit)
  2. I ___ Paris three times in my life. (visited, have visited, was visiting)
  3. When ___ you go there? (have, has, did)
  4. She hasn't finished ___. (yesterday, yet, last night)
  5. With finished time expressions (yesterday, in 2020, ago) we use past simple.
  6. 'I have seen him yesterday' is correct.
  7. Ben ___ (break) his arm last year.
  8. Ben ___ (break) his arm three times in his life.
  9. Kenji hasn't visited Cartagena ___.
  10. Unscramble: I, have, been, to, Madrid, twice
  11. Unscramble: when, did, you, go, there
  12. Find the mistake: I have visited Paris last year.
  13. Find the mistake: Did you ever been to Europe?
  14. Find the mistake: We have saw that movie already.
  15. You have been to Madrid. — Ask for the specific moment (use 'when' + past simple).
  16. Have you ever tried sushi? (Yes — last month) — Answer with the short answer + the detail in past simple.
  17. She has sent the email. — Write the negative form.
  18. They went out last night. — Write the negative form.
  19. He has already finished. — Write the negative form (use 'yet').
  20. Have you ever been to Madrid? (short answer)
  21. Did you study English yesterday? (short answer, No)

Listening

Sofía: Ben, have you found your wallet yet?

Ben: Yes, I have! I found it this morning. It was in the fridge.

Kenji: The fridge? How did it get there?

Ben: I put it there on Tuesday... next to the cheese. Don't ask.

Abuela Carmen: I have seen many strange things in my life, but I have never seen a wallet in a fridge.

Sofía: You have now, Abuela. You have now.

Reading

Sofía's client interview, part two. 'Have you ever worked with a difficult client?' they asked her. 'Yes, I have,' she said. 'Last year, a client changed the logo eleven times. He asked for more blue, but less blue. I finished the project in December, and I have never used that shade of blue again.' 'And have you ever missed a deadline?' 'No, I haven't. I have been a designer for seven years, and I delivered every single project on time. Even the blue one.'

  • Sofía has worked with a difficult client.
  • The client changed the logo three times.
  • She finished the blue project in December.
  • Sofía has missed many deadlines.
