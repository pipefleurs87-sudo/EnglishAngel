Have You Ever...? — Experiences (details in past simple)

B1 — grammar, vocabulary, listening, reading, writing, speaking

Kenji: Have you ever been to Europe, Sofía?

Sofía: Yes, I have. I went to Madrid in 2020, for a design conference.

Ben: Have you ever broken a bone? I have... I broke my arm last year. I fell off a hammock. A hammock, Kenji.

Abuela Carmen: I have never broken anything. But have you ever won a cumbia competition? I did — in 1975.

Common mistake: “Have you ever went to Europe?” — Correct: “Have you ever been to Europe?”

'Have you ever...?' asks if something happened in your life at least once — and when the answer is yes, you tell the details in past simple: Yes, I have. I went in 2020.

Practice

  1. Have you ever ___ to Europe? (been, went, go)
  2. Yes, I have. I ___ in 2020. (have gone, went, have been)
  3. I have ___ seen snow in my life. (ever, yet, never)
  4. After 'Yes, I have', we tell the details (when, where) in past simple.
  5. 'Ever' is used in affirmative sentences: 'I have ever been to Peru'.
  6. Has Ben ever ___ (break) a bone?
  7. Yes, he has. He ___ (break) his arm last year.
  8. Abuela Carmen won the competition. It ___ (be) in 1975.
  9. Unscramble: have, you, ever, sung, karaoke
  10. Unscramble: I, have, never, driven, a, motorcycle
  11. Find the mistake: Have you ever went to Europe?
  12. Find the mistake: Yes, I have. I have gone to Madrid in 2020.
  13. Find the mistake: She has never wons a prize.

  15. Have you ever seen snow? — Answer No with 'never'.
  16. Have you ever been to Europe? (Yes — Madrid, 2020) — Answer with the short answer + the detail in past simple.
  17. I have never sung karaoke. — Write the affirmative form.

Listening

Kenji: Question game! Have you ever sung karaoke, Sofía?

Sofía: Yes, I have. I sang 'Vivir Mi Vida' at my cousin's wedding in 2023. Twice.

Ben: Have you ever won anything, Kenji?

Kenji: Yes, I have! I won a photography contest when I was fifteen.

Sofía: And you, Abuela? Have you ever met anyone famous?

Abuela Carmen: Yes, I have. I met Gabriel García Márquez at a market in 1982. He bought mangoes. I sold them to him.
