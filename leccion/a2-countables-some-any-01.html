Countable & Uncountable — a / some / any / much / many (Food)

A2 — grammar, vocabulary, listening, reading, writing

Sofía: Let's cook! There's some cheese and there are some tomatoes in the fridge.

Ben: Bad news... there isn't any milk. And there aren't any eggs.

Kenji: Is there any rice left? I can make onigiri!

Abuela Carmen: Would you like some coffee while you decide? There's a lot of coffee in this house!

Common mistake: “We don't have some eggs.” — Correct: “We don't have any eggs.”

A/an, some and any talk about quantity without counting exactly — a/an for one countable thing, some for affirmatives and offers, any for negatives and questions.

a / an · some · any · much / many · a lot of — one at a time
WordUse it for...Example
a / anONE countable thing — 'a doctor' is any doctor; 'the doctor' is that specific oneThere is an apple. She is a doctor.
someaffirmative sentences — countable AND uncountableI need some sugar. I have some friends there.
some (offers)questions that offer or request politelyWould you like some coffee?
anynegatives and questions — countable AND uncountableIs there any milk left? We don't have any eggs.
muchuncountable — negatives and questionsThere isn't much bread. How much rice?
manycountable — negatives and questionsHow many apples are there?
a lot ofaffirmative sentences — both typesThere's a lot of juice.

Practice

  1. I need ___ sugar for the cake. (some, any, a)
  2. We don't have ___ eggs. (any, some, much)
  3. She is ___ doctor — I don't know her name. (a, the, an)
  4. When Abuela Carmen was young, there weren't ___ supermarkets in her town. (any, some, much)
  5. 'Milk' is uncountable, so we ask 'How much milk?', not 'How many milk?'.
  6. We use 'some' in negative sentences.
  7. Is there ___ milk left in the carton?
  8. Don't worry, there is ___ cheese in the fridge.
  9. How ___ apples are there in the basket?
  10. Unscramble: is, there, any, space, left, in, the, fridge
  11. Unscramble: would, you, like, some, coffee
  12. Find the mistake: We don't have some eggs.
  13. Find the mistake: There isn't many milk in the fridge.
  14. Find the mistake: There is a onion in the salad.
  15. There is some juice in the fridge. — Write the negative form.
  16. There are some tomatoes in the salad. — Write the yes/no question.
  17. Is there any rice left? — Answer with a short answer (Yes).
  18. There is a lot of bread. — Write the negative form (use 'much').
  19. There aren't any bananas. — Write the affirmative form (use 'some').
  20. Are there any apples in your fridge? (short answer)
  21. Is there any milk left? (short answer, No)

Listening

Sofía: What's in the fridge? We need to make dinner.

Ben: There's some cheese and there are some tomatoes. But there isn't any milk.

Kenji: Are there any eggs?

Ben: No, there aren't. And there isn't much rice either.

Sofía: OK — eggs, milk and rice. Anything else?

Abuela Carmen: Would you like some coffee before you go? There's a lot of coffee in this house!

Reading

Every Saturday, Abuela Carmen visits the market. She doesn't buy much: some fruit, some cheese, and a little bread. 'When I was a girl, there weren't any supermarkets,' she says. 'There was a market in the plaza, and there was a lot of noise! We didn't have much money, but there was always some food on the table.' Today there are three supermarkets in her neighborhood, but she still prefers the market. 'The supermarket tomatoes don't have any flavor,' she says. 'And at the market, my friend Rosa always gives me an extra apple.'

  • Abuela Carmen buys a lot of things at the market.
  • When she was a girl, there weren't any supermarkets.
  • Her family had a lot of money.
  • Rosa always gives her an extra apple.
