Past Simple — Regular Verbs (-ed) & Time Expressions

A2 — grammar, vocabulary, listening, reading, writing, speaking

Kenji: I visited the Gold Museum two days ago. It was amazing!

Sofía: A week ago I finished a big project. I celebrated with my family.

Ben: Last Christmas I stayed in a tiny hostel in Cartagena... I missed my family a lot.

Abuela Carmen: On my last birthday I danced all night. And last winter I traveled to the coast!

Common mistake: “I visit my grandma last week.” — Correct: “I visited my grandma last week.”

We use the past simple with -ed verbs to report finished actions at a specific past time — usually with a time expression like last night or two days ago.

-ed pronunciation: /t/ · /d/ · /ɪd/
SoundWhenExamples
/t/after soft (voiceless) sounds: p, k, s, sh, ch, fworked, watched, finished, danced
/d/after vowels and voiced soundsplayed, lived, enjoyed, studied
/ɪd/after t and d — adds an extra syllablevisited, wanted, needed, decided
  • /t/ worked — trabajó
  • /t/ watched — vio / miró
  • /t/ danced — bailó
  • /t/ finished — terminó
  • /d/ played — jugó / tocó
  • /d/ lived — vivió
  • /d/ enjoyed — disfrutó
  • /d/ studied — estudió
  • /ɪd/ visited — visitó
  • /ɪd/ wanted — quiso
  • /ɪd/ needed — necesitó
  • /ɪd/ decided — decidió
  • I watched a movie last night.
  • Sofía finished a big design project a week ago.
  • Kenji visited the Gold Museum two days ago.
  • Abuela Carmen danced all night on her last birthday.
  • Ben worked as a tour guide in Cartagena last winter.
  • I didn't work last Sunday. I stayed at home.
  • Sofía didn't travel last winter. She stayed in Medellín.
  • They didn't watch the game yesterday.
  • Kenji didn't study last night; he was very tired.
  • Did you watch the game last night?
  • Where did you travel last winter?
  • How did you celebrate your last birthday?
  • Did Kenji visit the museum two days ago?
  • Did you enjoy the party? Yes, I did.
  • Did Ben travel last Christmas? No, he didn't.
  • Did they play football yesterday? Yes, they did.

Practice

  1. I ___ a movie last night. (watched, watch, watches)
  2. How do you pronounce the -ed in 'wanted'? (/t/, /d/, /ɪd/)
  3. Kenji visited the museum ___. (two days ago, tomorrow, next week)
  4. The -ed in 'played' sounds /ɪd/, with an extra syllable.
  5. Time expressions like 'last night' and 'a week ago' usually go with the past simple.
  6. Sofía ___ (finish) her project a week ago.
  7. We ___ (dance) all night at the party last Christmas.
  8. They ___ (not / watch) the game yesterday.

Listening

Sofía: Kenji, what did you do last weekend?

Kenji: I visited a coffee farm near Medellín. I loved it!

Sofía: Did you try the coffee?

Kenji: Yes, I did! I talked with the farmers and watched the whole process.

Sofía: I stayed at home. I cleaned my apartment and watched a movie.

Kenji: Did you enjoy it?

Sofía: Yes, I did. Sometimes a quiet weekend is perfect.

Reading

Last Christmas, Ben stayed in Cartagena. He rented a small room in the old city and worked as a tour guide for two weeks. Every morning, he talked with tourists from many countries. Two days ago, he called his family in England: 'I missed you last Christmas,' he said, 'but I really enjoyed my time here. The people treated me like family.' Last night, Sofía and Kenji organized a small party for him, and they danced until midnight.

  • Ben stayed in Cartagena last Christmas.
  • Ben worked as a taxi driver.
  • Ben called his family two days ago.
  • The party finished early in the evening.
