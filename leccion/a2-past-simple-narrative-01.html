Past Simple — Telling a Story

A2 — grammar, vocabulary, listening, reading, writing

Common mistake: “Did Ben went to Cartagena?” — Correct: “Did Ben go to Cartagena?”

We use the past simple to tell finished actions in the past; after 'did' the verb goes back to its base form (go, not went).

Past simple in a story: did / didn't
FormStructureExample
Affirmativeverb in past form (went, took, lost)Ben went to Cartagena
Negativedid not (didn't) + base verbHe didn't bring a map
QuestionDid + subject + base verb?Did Ben enjoy the trip?
Short answerYes, he did. / No, he didn't.Did you go? Yes, I did.
Story connectorsfirst, then, because, so, luckilyHe lost his map, so he got lost

Practice

  1. Last year, Ben ___ to Cartagena. (go, went, goes)
  2. Ben ___ into the water because he was clumsy. (fell, falled, felled)
  3. He didn't ___ a map, so he got lost. (brought, bring, brings)
  4. After 'did' in a question, the main verb goes back to its base form: 'Did he go?', not 'Did he went?'.
  5. 'Ben buyed a hat' is correct because 'buy' is a regular verb.
  6. Ben ___ his hat when he fell into the water. (lose)
  7. He ___ not bring a map, so he got lost.
  8. Unscramble: met, Ben, a, family, local, .
  9. Find the mistake: Ben buyed a new hat at the market.
  10. Find the mistake: Did Ben went to Cartagena?
  11. Ben enjoyed the trip. — Turn it into a question (use 'did').
  12. Ben took a boat to the islands. — Make it negative (use 'did not').
  13. Where did you go on your last holiday? (Answer with a full sentence.)
  14. Ben lost his hat. — Write the negative form.
  15. Did Ben meet a local family? (short answer)

Listening

Kenji: Ben, tell me about your trip to Cartagena!

Ben: Oh, it was an adventure. I took a boat to the islands.

Kenji: Nice! Did you enjoy it?

Ben: Yes, but I fell into the water and I lost my hat.

Kenji: Oh no! What did you do?

Ben: I bought a new one and I met a wonderful local family.

Reading

Last year, Ben traveled to Cartagena. He was excited, but he was also very clumsy. He took a boat to the islands, and he fell into the water. He lost his hat and his map, so he got lost in the old town. Luckily, he met a local family. They helped him, and they ate arepas together on the beach. Ben did not speak Spanish well, but he tried, and he had a wonderful time.

  • Ben traveled to Cartagena last year.
  • Ben lost his hat and his map.
  • Ben spoke Spanish perfectly.
  • A local family helped Ben.
