Object Pronouns (me, him, her, us, them)

A1 — grammar, listening, reading, writing

Common mistake: “I love she.” — Correct: “I love her.”

We use object pronouns (me, you, him, her, it, us, them) after the verb or a preposition — they receive the action.

Subject -> Object
SubjectObjectExample
ImeSofía knows me
hehimWe know him
sheherI love her
weusThey help us
theythemI call them
  • Sofía knows me, and I love her.
  • They help us every day, so we thank them.
  • Ben doesn't understand them very well.
  • She doesn't call him on Sundays.
  • Do you know her? Does he like us?
  • Why does Kenji always ask me questions?
  • Do you know him? Yes, I do.
  • Does she call you? No, she doesn't.

Practice

  1. I see Ben. I see ___. (he, him, his)
  2. Sofía is nice. I like ___. (she, her, hers)
  3. Object pronouns (me, him, her...) come after the verb.
  4. Please call ___ tonight. (to me)
  5. We know Sofía and Ben. We know ___.
  6. Find the mistake: I love she.
  7. I like Sofía. — Replace 'Sofia' with an object pronoun.
  8. Sofía calls Ben. — Replace 'Ben' with an object pronoun.
  9. Do you know me? (Answer: Yes, I do. / No, I don't.)

Listening

Kenji: Sofía, do you know Ben's sister?

Sofía: Yes, I know her. She is very nice.

Kenji: Does she remember me?

Sofía: Of course she remembers you! We all like you.

Kenji: And Ben? Do you see him often?

Sofía: Yes, I see him every week in class.

Reading

Kenji is new, so he asks Sofía about everyone. 'Do you know that woman?' 'Yes, I know her.' 'And those men?' 'I know them too.' Kenji smiles. 'Everybody knows you, Sofía!' She laughs. 'Yes, and they like me because I help them. Friends help each other.'

  • Sofía knows the woman.
  • Sofía doesn't know the men.
  • People like Sofía because she helps them.
