Relative Clauses — Defining People (a person who...)

A1 — grammar, vocabulary, listening, reading, writing

Common mistake: “A carpenter is a person which works with wood.” — Correct: “A carpenter is a person who works with wood.”

We use 'who' to define a person by what they do: 'a person who + verb' (a person who works, who teaches, who helps).

  3. A carpenter is a person who ___ with wood.
  4. A baker is a person who ___ bread.
  5. A pilot is a person who ___ planes.
  6. A teacher is a person who ___ students.
  7. A mechanic is a person who ___ cars.

Listening

Kenji: Let's play a game! I describe a job, and you guess.

Ben: Okay, go!

Kenji: It's a person who works with wood.

Ben: That's easy — a carpenter!

Kenji: Yes! Now, a person who takes care of animals?

Ben: A vet! I love this game.

Reading

Kenji loves guessing games. 'A doctor is a person who helps sick people,' he says. 'A teacher is a person who teaches students, and a pilot is a person who flies planes.' Ben laughs. 'And you? What do you want to be?' Kenji thinks. 'I want to be an interpreter — a person who translates languages. That way, I can talk to everyone in the world!'

  • A pilot is a person who flies planes.
  • Kenji wants to be a doctor.
  • An interpreter is a person who translates languages.
