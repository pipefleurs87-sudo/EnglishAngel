Causative — Have / Get Something Done

B2 — grammar, vocabulary, writing, speaking

Kenji: Your new website looks amazing. Did you build it yourself?

Sofía: No, I had it designed by a friend — I just gave her the ideas.

Kenji: Smart. I always get my photos edited by someone else too, I have no eye for it.

Sofía: Exactly. You don't have to do everything yourself — just have it done well.

Common mistake: “I designed my logo by a professional.” — Correct: “I had my logo designed by a professional.”

Use have/get something done when someone else does a job for you — the subject arranges it, but doesn't do the action.

Who really does the action?
StructureExampleWho does it?
have + object + past participleI had my hair cutA hairdresser, not me
get + object + past participle (more informal)I got my car fixedA mechanic, not me

Practice

  1. Sofía ___ her website redesigned last month. (had, did, made)
  2. Ben gets his hair ___ every three weeks. (cut, cuts, cutting)
  3. We're having the office ___ this weekend. (painted, painting, paint)
  4. 'I had my logo designed' means I designed it myself.
  5. Causative 'have/get something done' uses the past participle of the verb.
  6. 'Get' is more informal than 'have' in this structure.
  7. Sofía had her website ___ (redesign) last month.
  8. We're getting the office ___ (paint) this weekend.
  9. They had their photos ___ (take) by a professional.
  10. Unscramble: Sofía, had, her, logo, designed, by, a, friend
  11. Unscramble: Ben, gets, his, hair, cut, every, month
  12. Find the mistake: I designed my logo by a professional.
  13. A friend designed Sofía's logo. — Rewrite using the causative (Sofía as subject).
  14. A mechanic fixed our car. — Rewrite using 'get' causative.
  15. Did Sofía have her website redesigned? — Answer with a short answer (Yes).
  16. Sofía had her logo designed by an agency. — Write the negative form.
  17. Do you get your hair cut often? (short answer, Yes)

Listening

Kenji: Your website looks so professional now.

Sofía: Thanks, I had it redesigned by a friend from design school.

Kenji: I need to get my resume looked at by someone too. I always mess up the formatting.

Sofía: I know someone who does that. She helped me get my portfolio organized last year.

Kenji: Perfect. I'll have her check it before I apply anywhere.

Reading

When Sofía started her studio, she tried to do everything herself — design, invoicing, even her own website. Three years later, she's learned to delegate. She had her website redesigned by a friend from design school, gets her invoices handled by an accountant twice a month, and even has her social media managed by an intern on weekends. 'I used to think doing it all myself made me a real professional,' she told Kenji. 'Now I know a real professional knows when to have something done by someone better at it.'

  • Sofía designed her own website when her studio started.
  • An accountant now handles her invoices.
  • Sofía manages her own social media every day.
  • Sofía believes real professionals never delegate.
