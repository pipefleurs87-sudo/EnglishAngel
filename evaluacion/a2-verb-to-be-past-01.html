Verb To Be — Past (was / were)

A2 — grammar, vocabulary, listening, reading, writing

Common mistake: “The players was sad after the game.” — Correct: “The players were sad after the game.”

We use was and were to describe and give opinions about past events, people, and situations — the past version of am / is / are.

was / were
SubjectTo be (past)Example
I / He / She / Itwas / wasn'tThe game was exciting
You / We / Theywere / weren'tThe players weren't happy
QuestionsWas / Were + subject...?Was the weekend good?
Short answersYes/No + pronoun + was(n't)/were(n't)Were you tired? Yes, I was.

Listening

Sofía: Hi Ben! How was your weekend?

Ben: It was fantastic! I was in Cartagena. The beach was beautiful.

Kenji: Were the hostels expensive?

Ben: No, they weren't. But they were full of tourists!

Sofía: And Kenji, how was the football game?

Kenji: Terrible. The players were so sad after the game. The final score was 3-0.

Reading

When Abuela Carmen was a girl, life was very different. There were no smartphones, and the streets were quiet. But life wasn't perfect in the past. For many people, like the slaves in colonial times, life was difficult and unfair. Later, in the 20th century, the world wasn't peaceful either: the Cold War was a long conflict between the USA and the USSR. Abuela Carmen says: 'The past wasn't better or worse — it was just different.'
