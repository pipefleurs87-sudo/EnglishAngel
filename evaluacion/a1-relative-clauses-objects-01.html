Relative Clauses — Objects to Work & Study (a thing that you use to...)

A1 — grammar, vocabulary, listening, reading, writing

Common mistake: “A pen is a thing who you use to write.” — Correct: “A pen is a thing that you use to write.”

We use 'which' or 'that' (not 'who') to define a thing by its use: 'a thing that you use to + verb'.

  • A computer is a thing which you use to work and study.
  • A ruler is not a thing that you use to write.
  • A printer isn't a thing you use to cook.

Practice

  1. A pen is a thing ___ you use to write. (who, that, where)
  2. We use 'which' or 'that' for things, not 'who'.
  3. A pen is a thing that you use to ___.
  4. A dictionary is a thing that you use to check ___.
  5. A printer is a thing that you use to ___ documents.
  6. A backpack is a thing that you use to ___ your books.
  7. Find the mistake: A computer is a thing who you use to work.
  8. A notebook. You use it to write notes. — Join them with 'that'.
  9. What do you call a thing that you use to write?

Listening

Kenji: Last round! A thing that you use to write.

Ben: A pen!

Kenji: Correct. A thing that you use to check words?

Ben: A dictionary!

Kenji: And a thing that you use to carry your books?

Ben: A backpack! I win the game.

Reading

On Sofía's desk there are many useful things. A pen is a thing that she uses to write, and a notebook is a thing that she uses to take notes. Her computer is a thing that she uses to work, and a dictionary is a thing that she uses to check English words. 'What is the most important thing on your desk?' asks Kenji. 'My coffee!' Sofía laughs. 'It's a thing that I use to wake up!'

  • A pen is a thing that Sofía uses to write.
  • Sofía uses a dictionary to cook.
  • Sofía jokes that coffee is a thing she uses to wake up.
