Past Simple — Irregular Verbs (patterns)

A2 — grammar, vocabulary, listening, reading, writing, speaking

Ben: I flew to Cartagena last winter — the ticket cost a fortune!

Kenji: I drank Colombian coffee for the first time a week ago. I thought it was perfect.

Sofía: I wrote three reports last night... and I slept only four hours.

Abuela Carmen: When I was young, I swam in the river and rode a horse to school!

Common mistake: “I buyed a new phone yesterday.” — Correct: “I bought a new phone yesterday.”

Irregular verbs make the past without -ed — each one changes its shape, but most follow a family pattern you can learn in groups.

Irregular patterns: base · past · participle

Practice

  1. Ben ___ to Cartagena last winter. (flew, flied, flyed)
  2. Which verb does NOT change in the past? (cut, drink, write)
  3. The past of 'buy' is ___. (bought, buyed, brought)
  4. 'Drink, drank, drunk' follows the i-a-u pattern, like 'sing' and 'swim'.
  5. The past of 'can' is 'could' or 'was able to'.
  6. Kenji ___ (drink) Colombian coffee a week ago.
  7. Sofía ___ (write) three reports last night.

Listening

Kenji: Ben, I heard you went to the beach yesterday!

Ben: Yes! I swam all morning. Then I ate fish and drank fresh juice.

Sofía: Did you take photos?

Ben: I took hundreds! But then I made a classic Ben mistake...

Kenji: Oh no. What did you do?

Ben: I left my phone on the bus. I had to buy a new one.

Abuela Carmen: Don't worry, Ben. When I was young, we couldn't even take photos!

Reading

Abuela Carmen grew up in a small town near the river. Life was simple: she rode a horse to school, swam in the river every summer, and drank fresh milk from her grandfather's farm. She couldn't travel much — a trip to the city cost a lot of money — but she never felt poor. 'We made our own fun,' she says. 'I sang with my sisters every night, and my mother taught me hundreds of songs. I forgot many things in my life, but I never forgot those songs.'

  • Abuela Carmen rode a horse to school.
  • She swam in the sea every summer.
  • A trip to the city was cheap.
  • Her mother taught her hundreds of songs.
