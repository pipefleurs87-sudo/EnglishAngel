Verb To Be — Nationalities

A1 — grammar, vocabulary, listening, reading, writing

Common mistake: “I from Colombia. I Colombian.” — Correct: “I am from Colombia. I am Colombian.”

We use the verb 'to be' (am / is / are) to say who you are, where you are from, and what you are like — your identity in one word.

Practice

  1. Sofía ___ from Medellín. (am, is, are)
  2. Ben is British. This means he is from ___. (Brazil, the United Kingdom, Japan)
  3. In English, nationalities are written with a capital letter: 'Japanese', not 'japanese'.
  4. Kenji and I ___ students. We love languages.
  5. Unscramble: is, Kenji, Japanese, .
  6. Find the mistake: She are Colombian.
  7. Ben is British. — Make it negative (use 'not').
  8. You are from Brazil. — Turn it into a question.
  9. Are you Colombian? (Answer: Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.)

Listening

Kenji: Hi! I am Kenji. I am from Japan.

Sofía: Nice to meet you, Kenji. I am Sofía. I am Colombian.

Kenji: Colombian? Are you from Bogotá?

Sofía: No, I am from Medellín.

Ben: Hello! I am Ben, and I am British.

Kenji: So we are all from different countries!

Reading

Sofía, Ben, and Kenji are friends. Sofía is Colombian; she is from Medellín. Ben is British; he is from London. Kenji is Japanese. They are not from the same country, but they are all in Colombia now. They are happy together.

  • Sofía is from Bogotá.
  • Ben is British.
  • Kenji is from Colombia.
  • The three friends are in Colombia now.
