Wh- Questions — Question Bank (you / he-she)

A1 — grammar, listening, reading, writing

Common mistake: “Where he works?” — Correct: “Where does he work?”

Each wh- word asks for different information; in the present simple we use 'do' (you/we/they) and 'does' (he/she/it) + the base verb.

One question with each wh- (you / he-she)

  • What do you do? What does she do?
  • Where do you live? Where does he live?
  • When do you start work? When does the class start?
  • Who do you live with? Who does he know here?
  • Why do you study English? Why does she travel so much?
  • How do you feel today? How does he go to work?
  • How often do you exercise? How often does she call her family?
  • What time does the film start?
  • How much does it cost? How many languages does Kenji speak?

Practice

  1. ___ do you live? — In Medellín. (What, Where, When)
  2. ___ does she start work? — At eight. (Where, When, Who)
  3. With he/she/it we use 'does': 'What does he do?'.
  4. ___ do you feel today? — I feel great. (how)
  5. Why ___ she travel so much? (do/does)
  6. How ___ languages does Kenji speak? (quantity)
  7. Find the mistake: Where he works?
  8. She lives in Bogotá. — Ask about the place (Where + does).
  9. He goes to work by bus. — Ask about the manner (How + does).
  10. Write a 'What' question about your father (3rd person).

Listening

Kenji: I love asking questions! Where do you live, Ben?

Ben: I live in London.

Kenji: What do you do there?

Ben: I work in a bookshop.

Kenji: How often do you travel?

Ben: Twice a year. Why do you ask so many questions?!

Reading

Kenji is curious about everyone. To his teacher he asks: 'What do you do at the weekend? Where do you go? Who do you meet?' To his friend he asks: 'What does Ben do? Where does he live? How often does he travel?' His questions never stop. 'You know,' says Sofía, 'a good question opens a door. And you, Kenji, open a hundred doors a day!'

  • Kenji asks a lot of questions.
  • Kenji never asks about his friends.
  • Sofía says a good question opens a door.
