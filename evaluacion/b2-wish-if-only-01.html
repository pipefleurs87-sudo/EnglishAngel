Wish / If Only — Regrets & Change

B2 — grammar, speaking, writing

Ben: I wish I hadn't taken my phone into the water.

Abuela Carmen: I wish you would listen to me before, not after.

Kenji: I wish I spoke Spanish as well as I understand it.

Abuela Carmen: If only wishing fixed phones and grammar both.

Common mistake: “I wish I have more free time.” — Correct: “I wish I had more free time.”

Wish and if only express that we want something to be different from how it really is — right now (past simple) or in the past (past perfect).

Practice

  1. I wish I ___ more free time. (had, have, having)
  2. Ben wishes he ___ bought that case. (had, has, have)
  3. I wish he ___ call more often. (would, will, wish)
  4. 'I wish I have more time' is correct.
  5. 'Wish + past perfect' talks about a past regret.
  6. 'If only' can be used the same way as 'I wish'.
  7. I wish I ___ (have) more free time.
  8. Ben wishes he ___ (not/take) his phone into the water.
  9. If only Kenji ___ (speak) Spanish more fluently.
  10. Unscramble: I, wish, I, had, more, free, time
  11. Unscramble: Ben, wishes, he, hadn't, taken, his, phone
  12. Find the mistake: I wish I have more free time.
  13. I don't have much free time. I want it to be different. — Rewrite as a wish.
  14. Ben took his phone into the water. He regrets it. — Rewrite as a wish about the past.
  15. Do you wish you had more time? — Answer with a short answer (Yes).
  16. I wish I had more free time. — Write the opposite wish (you have too much free time).
  17. Do you wish you had studied a different career? (short answer, No)

Listening

Ben: I wish I hadn't taken my phone into the water.

Abuela Carmen: I wish you would learn from your mistakes a little faster.

Kenji: I wish I spoke Spanish as well as I understand it — everyone talks so fast.

Sofía: If only wishing actually fixed anything. Come on, let's get you a new case, Ben.

Ben: I wish today had gone differently, honestly.

Sofía: Don't we all, Ben. Don't we all.

Reading

Everyone in the group has a wish they repeat like a small prayer. Ben wishes he hadn't taken his phone into the water — again — and says it every time with the same tired honesty. Kenji wishes his spoken Spanish matched his listening; he understands almost everything now, but the words still arrive too slowly when it's his turn to speak. Sofía wishes she had more hours in the day, though she admits that if she had them, she'd probably just fill them with more work. Abuela Carmen just wishes the three of them would learn a little faster — from their mistakes, from each other, from her stories. 'If only wishing did the learning for you,' she says, 'I'd have retired years ago.'

  • Kenji says his spoken Spanish is better than his listening.
  • Sofía believes extra hours would mostly go to more work.
