Phrasal Verbs with UP

B2 — vocabulary, speaking, listening, reading

Sofía: You woke up late again, didn't you?

Ben: I didn't wake up late, I just showed up late. Different problem.

Kenji: Either way, we ended up starting the class without you.

Ben: I'll look up what I missed. I promise I won't give up on this course.

Common mistake: “I looked up and saw the word in the sky.” — Correct: “I looked up the word in the dictionary.”

Phrasal verbs with 'up' rarely mean 'upward' — they usually describe completing, increasing, or discovering something.

UP — completing, increasing, discovering
Phrasal verbMeaningExample
wake upstop sleepingI woke up at 7
grow upbecome an adultShe grew up in Medellín
give upstop tryingDon't give up!
look upsearch for informationLook it up online
show uparrive, appearHe showed up an hour late
end upreach an unexpected final situationWe ended up walking home
  • despertar wake up — despertarse
  • crecer/madurar grow up — crecer / madurar
  • rendirse give up — rendirse
  • buscar información look up — buscar (diccionario/internet)
  • aparecer/llegar show up — aparecer / llegar
  • terminar inesperadamente end up — terminar (en algo inesperado)

  • They ended up staying until midnight.

  • We didn't clean up after the party.

Practice

  1. Ben ___ late again this morning. (woke up, grew up, gave up)
  2. Kenji always ___ new words he doesn't know. (looks up, shows up, cleans up)
  3. They ___ staying until midnight. (ended up, gave up, woke up)
  4. 'Look up' usually means to search for information.
  5. 'Give up' means to try harder.
  6. 'Show up' means to arrive somewhere.
  7. Sofía ___ (grow, past) up in Medellín.
  8. We ___ (not/clean) up after the party.
  9. Did Ben ___ up eventually?
  10. Unscramble: Ben, woke, up, late, again
  11. Unscramble: they, ended, up, staying, until, midnight
  12. Find the mistake: I looked up and saw the word in the dictionary.
  13. Ben arrived late to the meeting. — Rewrite using 'show up'.
  14. Sofía never stops trying. — Rewrite using 'give up' (negative).
  15. Did Ben show up late? — Answer with a short answer (Yes).
  16. Ben showed up on time. — Write the negative form.
  17. What time did you wake up today?

Listening

Sofía: You woke up late again, I can tell.

Ben: I showed up on time, that's what matters.

Kenji: You ended up missing the first ten minutes.

Ben: I'll look up what I missed and catch up.

Sofía: Just don't give up on the whole course because of ten minutes.

Ben: Never. I'm growing up, slowly.

Reading

Kenji grew up in Osaka, in a house where everyone woke up before sunrise. In Medellín, he's slowly given up on that habit — mornings here feel different, slower. He still looks up unfamiliar words constantly, filling his notebook with phrases he hears from Ben and Sofía. Sometimes he shows up to class early just to practice with whoever's there. Other times, a good conversation with Abuela Carmen means he ends up staying for hours, notebook forgotten on the table. He hasn't given up on becoming fluent — if anything, moving here just proved how much further he still has to go, and how much he enjoys the trip.

  • Kenji grew up in Osaka.
  • He has completely stopped waking up early since moving.
  • Kenji sometimes ends up staying for hours talking with Abuela Carmen.
  • Kenji has given up on becoming fluent.
