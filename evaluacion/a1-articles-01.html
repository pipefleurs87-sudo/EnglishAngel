Articles — a / an / the

A1 — grammar, listening, reading, writing

Sofía: I am a designer. Ben is a photographer... an unlucky photographer.

Ben: Hey! I saw an iguana this morning. It was in the garden, next to the mango tree.

Kenji: The sun is strong today. I need a hat and an umbrella.

Abuela Carmen: In this house there is a rule: the last arepa is for the grandmother.

Common mistake: “She is teacher.” — Correct: “She is a teacher.”

Articles present a noun: a/an for one thing among many (any teacher), the for the specific one everybody knows (the sun) — and English needs them even where Spanish doesn't.

a / an / the
ArticleUse it for...Example
aone thing — before consonant SOUNDa banana, a hat, a university
anone thing — before vowel SOUNDan apple, an iguana, an hour
thethe specific or unique onethe sun, the last arepa, the garden
a + jobEnglish needs it: She is A teacherHe is an engineer
no articlegeneralizations in pluralI like apples (not 'the apples')
  • I have an apple and a banana.
  • She is a teacher at the school.
  • The sun is very strong today.
  • There is an iguana in the garden.
  • He isn't a doctor; he is an engineer.
  • I don't have a car.
  • This isn't the correct answer.
  • Is there a supermarket near here?
  • Do you have an umbrella?
  • Is she a designer?
  • Is she a teacher? Yes, she is.
  • Do you have a pet? No, I don't.
  • Is this the last arepa? Yes, it is... and it's mine.

Practice

  1. I have ___ apple. (an, a, the)
  2. She is ___ teacher. (the, a, —)
  3. ___ sun is strong today. (A, An, The)
  4. We choose 'an' by the sound, not the letter: an apple, an hour.
  5. In English, jobs don't need an article: 'She is doctor' is correct.
  6. There is ___ iguana in the garden. (a / an / the)
  7. Kenji needs ___ hat for the sun. (a / an / the)
  8. ___ last arepa is for the grandmother. (a / an / the)
  9. Unscramble: she, is, a, teacher, at, the, school
  10. Unscramble: is, there, a, supermarket, near, here
  11. Find the mistake: I have a apple.
  12. Find the mistake: She is teacher.
  13. Find the mistake: An sun is very strong today.
  14. She is a designer. — Write the negative form.
  15. There is an iguana in the garden. — Write the yes/no question.
  16. Is she a teacher? — Answer with a short answer (Yes).
  17. I have a car. — Write the negative form.
  18. He isn't an engineer. — Write the affirmative form.
  19. Do you have a pet? (short answer)
  20. Is the sun a planet? (short answer, No)

Listening

Kenji: Sofía, is there a supermarket near here?

Sofía: Yes, there is one on the corner, next to the park.

Kenji: Great. I need an umbrella and a hat. The sun is terrible today.

Ben: Buy an ice cream too! There is a machine at the entrance.

Sofía: Ben, you are a genius... an ice cream genius.

Ben: It's a gift.

Reading

Kenji writes in his diary: 'Today I saw an iguana for the first time. It was in the garden of the house, under a mango tree. Abuela Carmen says the iguana visits every morning and eats a flower — always one flower, always at the same time. It is a very organized animal. In Japan, I had a cat. A cat is a good friend, but an iguana with a schedule? That is a Colombian surprise.'

  • Kenji saw an iguana in the garden.
  • The iguana eats three flowers every day.
  • Kenji had a cat in Japan.
  • The iguana visits at a different time every day.
