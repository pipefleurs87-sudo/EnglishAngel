Present Continuous — Now (vs Present Simple)

A1 — grammar, vocabulary, listening, reading, writing

Common mistake: “Right now, Sofía cooks dinner.” — Correct: “Right now, Sofía is cooking dinner.”

We use the present continuous (am/is/are + verb-ing) for actions happening now; the present simple for routines and things that are always true.

Present Simple (always) vs Present Continuous (now)
Present Simple — alwaysPresent Continuous — now
What do you do?What are you doing now?
I work in a school.I am reading a book.
What does your father do?What is your father doing now?
He is a doctor.He is cooking dinner.
  • reading — leyendo
  • cooking — cocinando
  • working — trabajando
  • sleeping — durmiendo
  • studying — estudiando
  • running — corriendo
  • watching TV — viendo tele
  • playing — jugando
  • Sofía is working now, and the children are playing.
  • Look! Ben is reading and Kenji is cooking.
  • Ben isn't sleeping; he is reading.
  • The kids aren't studying right now.
  • What are you doing? Is Kenji studying now?
  • What is your father doing now?
  • Are you working now? Yes, I am.
  • Is she sleeping? No, she isn't.

Practice

  1. Look! The baby ___. (cries, is crying, cry)
  2. Every day Sofía ___ to work, but today she is staying home. (goes, is going, go)
  3. We use present continuous (am/is/are + -ing) for actions happening now.
  4. Be quiet! The baby ___ (sleep) right now.
  5. Sofía usually cooks dinner, but tonight Ben ___ (cook).
  6. Find the mistake: She is cook dinner.
  7. Find the mistake: What does your father doing now?
  8. You are working. — Turn it into a question.
  9. Sofía reads every day. — Change it to NOW (present continuous).
  10. What are you doing right now?

Listening

Kenji: Ben, what do you do?

Ben: I work in a bookshop.

Kenji: And what are you doing now?

Ben: Right now, I am drinking coffee and reading.

Kenji: Sofía usually cooks on Sundays. What is she doing now?

Ben: Ha! Right now she is dancing in the kitchen!

Reading

There are two ways to talk about the present. Present simple is for your life in general: 'Sofía works in Medellín. Her father is a doctor.' Present continuous is for this exact moment: 'Right now, Sofía is drinking coffee, and her father is examining a patient.' See the difference? 'What do you do?' asks about your job. 'What are you doing?' asks about now. Two questions, two answers!

  • Present continuous is for this exact moment.
  • 'What do you do?' asks about right now.
  • Present simple is for your life in general.
