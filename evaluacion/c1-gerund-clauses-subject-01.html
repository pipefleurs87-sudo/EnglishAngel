Gerund Clauses as Subject

C1 — grammar, writing, speaking

Sofía: Your finishing the report early really impressed the client.

Kenji: Honestly, me finishing early surprised me too.

Ben: Ben losing his phone again surprised absolutely nobody, for the record.

Abuela Carmen: At least you're self-aware. That's something.

Common mistake: “Ben lose his phone again surprised no one.” — Correct: “Ben losing his phone again surprised no one.”

A gerund clause can act as the whole subject of a sentence — formally with a possessive ('your finishing') or, much more commonly in speech, with the plain pronoun or noun ('you finishing').

Practice

  1. Ben ___ his phone again surprised no one. (losing, lose, lost)
  2. ___ finishing the project early impressed the client. (Your, You are, You've)
  3. Kenji ___ every night finally paid off. (studying, study, studied)
  4. A gerund clause can function as the subject of a sentence.
  5. 'Your finishing early' is the more formal version of 'you finishing early'.
  6. Gerund clauses as subject always need the base form of the verb, not -ing.
  7. Sofía's ___ (take) on extra clients worried Abuela Carmen a little.
  8. Ben ___ (not/check) the weather caused the whole problem.
  9. Your ___ (not/call) me back was a bit worrying.
  10. Unscramble: Ben, losing, his, phone, again, surprised, no, one
  11. Unscramble: your, finishing, the, project, early, impressed, the, client
  12. Find the mistake: Ben lose his phone again surprised no one.
  13. The fact that Ben lost his phone again surprised no one. — Rewrite using a gerund clause as subject.
  14. The fact that you finished early impressed the client. — Rewrite using a gerund clause (formal, possessive).
  15. Did Kenji's studying pay off? — Answer with a short answer (Yes).
  16. Ben checking the weather prevented the problem. — Write the negative form.
  17. Does someone constantly being late bother you? (short answer)

Listening

Sofía: Your finishing the report early really impressed the client.

Kenji: Honestly, me finishing early surprised me too.

Ben: Ben losing his phone again surprised absolutely nobody, for the record.

Sofía: At least you know yourself, Ben.

Ben: Self-awareness is my whole personality at this point.

Kenji: Your saying that out loud is somehow both funny and sad.

Reading

Sofía has started keeping a private list of things that no longer surprise her. Ben losing his phone tops it, followed closely by him arriving fifteen minutes late to everything that isn't actually urgent. Kenji finishing his assignments early, on the other hand, still catches her off guard every time — his studying every single night finally paying off feels less like a surprise and more like something she should have expected from the start. Your growing used to someone, she's realized, changes what surprises you about them. Their being exactly who they always were stops being news, and starts being comfort.
