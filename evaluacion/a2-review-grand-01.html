Practice

  1. Ben ___ in the sea when a big wave took his phone. (was swimming, swam, is swimming)
  2. ___ two beds in the room. (There is, There are, There was)
  3. The beach was ___ than last year. (crowded, more crowded, most crowded)
  4. 'The players was sad' is correct.
  5. We use 'going to' for a plan we already decided.
  6. There ___ much water in the mini fridge, so they bought some juice.
  7. Kenji ___ (fly) to Bogotá and thought the food was amazing.
  8. Next month, they ___ (go) to visit Kenji in Japan.
  9. Unscramble: a, There, balcony, small, was
  10. Unscramble: dinner, when, called, I, having, Dani, was
  11. Find the mistake: The weekend were fantastic.
  12. Find the mistake: The food was more cheap than the hotel.
  13. Find the mistake: There is many hotels in Cartagena.
  14. Sofía finishes her design project. — Put it in the past (it happened a week before the trip).
  15. The hotel room is small. — Compare it with 'the beach house' (bigger).
  16. The hotel was cheap. — Write the negative.
  17. What are you going to do next weekend?
  18. What were you doing yesterday at 8 pm, and what happened?

Listening

Ben: I have to tell you about my trip to Cartagena last month.

Sofía: Oh no, what happened this time?

Ben: I was swimming in the sea when a big wave took my phone. It just disappeared!

Kenji: That's terrible! Was the hotel at least nice?

Ben: It was okay. There were two beds and a small balcony, but there wasn't much water in the mini fridge.

Sofía: Well, next time you're going to bring a waterproof bag.

Ben: You're right. I'm going to buy one this week.

Reading

It is the end of a busy month for everyone. Ben's trip to Cartagena wasn't perfect — he lost his phone in the sea, but the food was better than he expected. Sofía finished her biggest project yet: a logo for a client who wanted 'more blue, but less blue' at the same time. There wasn't much time to rest, but there was always coffee. Next month, the friends are going to meet in Bogotá, and this time, Ben is going to bring a waterproof bag. Some things change; others, like Ben's bad luck with phones, probably won't.
