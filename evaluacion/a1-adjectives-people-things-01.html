Adjectives — Describing People & Things

A1 — grammar, vocabulary, listening, reading, writing

Kenji: Describe the team! Sofía is a smart designer and a fast worker.

Sofía: Kenji is a friendly student with big questions.

Ben: And I am a funny photographer! ...Why is everybody quiet?

Abuela Carmen: You are a good boy, Ben. And I am an old woman with strong opinions.

Common mistake: “They are students talls.” — Correct: “They are tall students.”

Adjectives describe people and things — in English they go BEFORE the noun and never take a plural: tall students, not 'students talls'.

Practice

  1. Medellín is a ___ city. (beautiful, beautifully, beauty)
  2. They are ___ students. (talls, tall, the tall)
  3. The opposite of 'big' is ___. (strong, short, small)
  4. In English, adjectives go before the noun: 'a smart designer'.
  5. Adjectives take -s in plural: 'funnies friends'.
  6. Abuela Carmen is old, but she is very ___ (fuerte).
  7. Kenji is a ___ (amigable) person.
  8. My grandfather is ___ (viejo), and my baby sister is young.
  9. Unscramble: Sofía, is, a, smart, designer
  10. Unscramble: is, your, city, big, or, small
  11. Find the mistake: They are students talls.
  12. Find the mistake: She has a car fast.
  13. Find the mistake: My friends are funnies.
  14. Ben is a fast runner. — Write the negative form.
  15. Kenji is a friendly person. — Write the yes/no question.
  16. Is she smart? — Answer with a short answer (Yes).
  17. The house is big. — Write the opposite (use 'small').
  18. They aren't tall. — Write the affirmative form.
  19. Are your friends funny? (short answer)
  20. Is an ant big? (short answer, No)

Listening

Sofía: Kenji, describe your family!

Kenji: My father is tall and quiet. My mother is small and very smart.

Ben: And your grandmother?

Kenji: She is old and funny — like Abuela Carmen, but with a small dog.

Abuela Carmen: A small dog? I have a big kitchen. That is better.

Sofía: Different countries, same grandmothers: strong and funny.

Reading

Welcome to the team! Sofía is a young designer from Medellín. She is smart, fast, and very organized. Ben is a tall photographer from London. He is friendly and funny, but he is a little clumsy — it's part of his charm. Kenji is a curious student from Japan. He is quiet at first, but he asks big questions. And Abuela Carmen? She is an old woman with a young heart, a big kitchen, and the best arepas in the neighborhood.

  • Sofía is a young designer.
  • Ben is short and serious.
  • Kenji asks big questions.
  • Abuela Carmen has a small kitchen.
