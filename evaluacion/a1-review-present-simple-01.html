A1 Review — Present Simple: Routines, Frequency, Questions & Likes

A1 — grammar, listening, reading, writing

Common mistake: “Sofía works always in the morning.” — Correct: “Sofía always works in the morning.”

The present simple, with frequency adverbs, questions and likes, is how we describe the life we live every day — routines, habits and tastes.

present simple — the complete map
UseExample
AffirmativeShe works every day
NegativeShe doesn't work on Sundays
QuestionDoes she work here?
Wh- questionWhere does she work?
FrequencyShe always works early
LikesShe loves designing
  • Sofía always gets up at six and designs posters every day.
  • Kenji doesn't understand Colombian jokes, but he always laughs.
  • Does Carmen cook every day? Yes, she does.
  • Where does Ben live? He never remembers his own address.
  • Sofía loves dancing salsa, but she hates getting up late.
  • Ben usually plays football on Sundays.

Practice

  1. Kenji ___ up at 6:00 every day. (gets, get, getting)
  2. ___ Sofía like coffee? (Does, Do, Is)
  3. Ben ___ like mornings. He wakes up late. (doesn't, don't, isn't)
  4. 'Does she works here?' is correct.
  5. Adverbs like 'always' go before the main verb.
  6. Sofía ___ (design) posters every week.
  7. What time ___ Kenji start class?
  8. Carmen never ___ (watch) TV at night.
  9. Unscramble: usually, Sofía, early, gets up
  10. Unscramble: does, live, Where, Kenji
  11. Find the mistake: Kenji don't understand the joke.
  12. Find the mistake: Ben plays never football.
  13. Find the mistake: Sofía likes dance salsa.
  14. Sofía works in Medellín. — Turn it into a yes/no question.
  15. Kenji likes sushi. — Make it negative.
  16. Carmen always cooks dinner. — Write the opposite frequency (never).
  17. What do you usually do on Sundays? (I usually...)
  18. What does your best friend love doing? (He/She loves + -ing)

Listening

Kenji: Sofía, what time do you get up?

Sofía: I always get up at six. I love the quiet morning.

Kenji: Six?! Ben never gets up before ten.

Sofía: I know! And he doesn't eat breakfast. He just runs to class.

Kenji: What does Carmen do in the morning?

Sofía: She waters her plants and cooks arepas. She never changes her routine.

Reading

Sofía's week is a perfect machine. She always gets up at six, drinks one black coffee and designs until noon. She doesn't work on Sundays — Sundays are for salsa. Kenji admires her discipline, but he doesn't copy it: he studies late and asks questions all day. 'Why does she never arrive late?' he wonders. Ben has a different theory: 'She is not human.' Carmen just smiles. She knows the secret: her granddaughter loves what she does.

  • Sofía works on Sundays.
  • Kenji studies late.
  • Sofía loves her work.
