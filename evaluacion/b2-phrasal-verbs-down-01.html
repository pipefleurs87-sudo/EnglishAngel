Phrasal Verbs with DOWN

B2 — vocabulary, speaking, listening, reading

Ben: The car broke down again. Third time this month.

Sofía: Calm down, we'll figure it out.

Ben: I am calm! I just need to write down the mechanic's number.

Kenji: Maybe you should turn down every trip that involves a car from now on.

Common mistake: “She turned down the job offer because it went underground.” — Correct: “She turned down the job offer because the salary was too low.”

Phrasal verbs with 'down' often describe reducing, refusing, breaking, or recording something — not literally moving downward.

DOWN — reducing, refusing, breaking, recording
Phrasal verbMeaningExample
calm downbecome less angry/upsetCalm down, it's okay
let downdisappointDon't let me down
break downstop working (machine) / lose emotional controlThe car broke down
turn downrefuse an offerShe turned down the job
write downnote something on paperWrite down the address
slow downreduce speedSlow down, it's icy

Practice

  1. Sofía ___ the first offer she received. (turned down, wrote down, calmed down)
  2. Ben's car ___ on the way to the coast. (broke down, let down, slowed down)
  3. Kenji always ___ new phrasal verbs. (writes down, turns down, breaks down)
  4. 'Turn down' means to accept an offer.
  5. 'Break down' can describe a machine that stops working.
  6. 'Calm down' means to become more upset.
  7. You should ___ down before the curve.
  8. Sofía didn't let her client ___.
  9. I didn't ___ down the address.
  10. Unscramble: Ben's, car, broke, down, again
  11. Unscramble: Sofía, turned, down, the, first, offer
  12. Find the mistake: She turned up the job offer because the salary was too low.
  13. Sofía refused the first offer. — Rewrite using 'turn down'.
  14. The car stopped working on the way to the coast. — Rewrite using 'break down'.
  15. Did the car break down again? — Answer with a short answer (Yes).
  16. Sofía let her client down. — Write the negative form.
  17. Have you ever turned down a job offer? (short answer)

Listening

Ben: The car broke down right before the bridge.

Sofía: Calm down, we'll call someone.

Ben: I am calm. I already wrote down the mechanic's number.

Kenji: Maybe it's time to turn down every plan involving your car.

Ben: Harsh, but maybe fair.

Reading

Ben's car has broken down four times this year, and each time follows the same pattern: he calls Sofía, she tells him to calm down, and he insists he already is. Last month he finally wrote down every mechanic's number he's ever used, just in case. Kenji has suggested, more than once, that Ben turn down any trip that requires driving more than twenty minutes. Ben always refuses — not because he loves the car, but because he refuses to let his own bad luck slow him down completely.

  • Ben's car has broken down four times this year.
  • Ben always admits he isn't calm.
  • Kenji has suggested Ben avoid long drives.
  • Ben has decided to stop driving completely.
