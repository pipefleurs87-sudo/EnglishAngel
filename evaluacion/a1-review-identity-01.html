A1 Review — Identity: To Be, Pronouns & Demonstratives

A1 — grammar, listening, reading, writing

Common mistake: “This is Sofía. Her is from Colombia.” — Correct: “This is Sofía. She is from Colombia.”

We combine 'to be', pronouns and this/that/these/those to introduce and identify people and things — the language of meeting someone.

Practice

  1. ___ is my friend Kenji. He is from Japan. (This, These, Those)
  2. Sofía and I ___ designers. ___ office is in Medellín. (are / Our, is / Our, are / We)
  3. That is Kenji's camera. ___ is Japanese. (It, He, This)
  4. 'These' is for one thing near you.
  5. 'They' can replace 'Kenji and Ben'.
  6. This ___ my grandmother, Carmen.
  7. Those women are teachers. ___ names are Ana and Luz.
  8. Kenji is my friend. ___ is from Tokyo.
  9. Unscramble: is, This, sister, my
  10. Unscramble: are, from, We, Colombia
  11. Find the mistake: This is Sofía. Her is a designer.
  12. Find the mistake: These is my parents.
  13. Find the mistake: I am Kenji. Mi family is in Japan.
  14. Sofía is Colombian. — Make it negative.
  15. This is your book. — Turn it into a question.
  16. This is my desk. — Write the opposite: near → far.
  17. Who is your best friend? Answer with 'My best friend is...'
  18. Introduce yourself: name and nationality. (I am... / My name is...)

Listening

Sofía: Abuela, this is my friend Kenji. He is from Japan.

Carmen: Welcome, Kenji! Our house is your house.

Kenji: Thank you! Is this your garden? It is beautiful.

Carmen: Yes, it is. These flowers are my treasure.

Kenji: Your garden is amazing. My grandmother in Japan is a gardener too!

Carmen: Then we are friends already.

Reading

This is our group. Sofía is a designer from Medellín, and her desk is always perfectly organized. Kenji is a student from Japan; his questions are famous in class. Ben is British — those big boots by the door are his. And that wise woman in the kitchen? She is Carmen, Sofía's grandmother. Their house is small, but their hearts are enormous. We are not just classmates. We are a family.
