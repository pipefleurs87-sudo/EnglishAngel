Passive Voice — Present, Past & Perfect

B1 — grammar, vocabulary, listening, reading, writing

Kenji: Did you know? Coffee is grown in 22 departments of Colombia. I read it in a museum!

Sofía: This logo was designed by me... and it is being changed by the client. Again.

Ben: My phone was stolen! ...No, wait. It has been found. It was in my pocket.

Abuela Carmen: In this house, dinner is served at seven. The rule was made in 1985, and it has never been broken.

Common mistake: “This house built in 1980.” — Correct: “This house was built in 1980.”

The passive voice puts the spotlight on what receives the action, not on who does it — and it always needs two pieces: the verb 'to be' + a past participle.

One sentence through the tenses: coffee + grow
TensePassive = be + participleExample
Present simpleis / are + grownCoffee is grown in Colombia
Present continuousis / are being + grownA new blend is being grown this year
Past simplewas / were + grownThis coffee was grown by my grandfather
Past continuouswas / were being + grownThe beans were being grown organically
Present perfecthas / have been + grownCoffee has been grown here since 1900
Active vs Passivedoer in focus → receiver in focusFarmers grow coffee → Coffee is grown (by farmers)

Practice

  1. Coffee ___ in Colombia. (is grown, is grow, grows itself)
  2. This house ___ in 1980. (is being built, was built, has built)
  3. The email hasn't ___ yet. (being sent, be sent, been sent)
  4. Right now, the logo ___ by the client. (is being changed, is changed, was changed)
  5. The passive voice always needs the verb 'to be' + a past participle.
  6. 'The window broke by the ball' is a correct passive sentence.
  7. Spanish ___ (speak) in Colombia. — present simple passive
  8. The beans ___ (dry) when it started to rain. — past continuous passive
  9. Coffee ___ (grow) here since 1900. — present perfect passive

  11. Unscramble: was, this, house, built, in, 1980

Listening

Kenji: Welcome to my coffee tour! First fact: coffee was brought to Colombia in the 18th century.

Sofía: Nice fact. Where is it grown today?

Kenji: It is grown in 22 departments. And the best beans are picked by hand, one by one.

Ben: And then what happens?

Kenji: The beans are dried in the sun, and the coffee is exported to the whole world.

Abuela Carmen: And in this house, it has been prepared the same way since 1985. Cups are on the table!

Reading

The old house on the corner has a story. It was built in 1923 by an Italian architect who never saw it finished. For decades, it was used as a school, and thousands of children were taught there — Abuela Carmen was one of them. Then it was abandoned for twenty years. Today, everything is different: the house is being restored by a group of young artists. The walls have been painted, the garden has been replanted, and a new roof is being installed this month. 'Beautiful things are not destroyed,' says Abuela Carmen. 'They are just forgotten for a while.'

  • The house was built by an Italian architect.
  • It was used as a hospital for decades.
  • The house is being restored by young artists.
  • The garden hasn't been replanted yet.
