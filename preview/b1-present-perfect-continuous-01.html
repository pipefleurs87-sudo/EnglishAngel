Present Perfect Continuous — for / since / just

B1 — grammar, vocabulary, listening, reading, writing

Sofía: I have been designing the same logo for two weeks. The client wants it 'more blue, but less blue'.

Kenji: I have been studying Spanish since January. Today I learned that 'ahorita' can mean any moment in history.

Abuela Carmen: I have been cooking since 6 a.m. — Ben, have you finished setting the table?

Ben: Yes, I have! I just did. ...OK, OK, I'm doing it now.

Common mistake: “I have been working here since three years.” — Correct: “I have been working here for three years.”

The present perfect continuous (have been + -ing) measures how long something has been happening — 'since' marks the starting point, 'for' the amount of time, and 'just' says it finished a moment ago.

  • since since 2020 — desde 2020 (punto de inicio)
  • since since Monday — desde el lunes
  • since since 8 a.m. — desde las 8 a.m.
  • since since I was a child — desde que era niño/a
  • for for two hours — durante dos horas (rango)
  • for for three days — durante tres días
  • for for ten years — durante diez años
  • for for a long time — durante mucho tiempo

Listening

Kenji: Sofía, how long have you been working as a designer?

Sofía: I have been designing since 2019 — so, for about seven years.

Kenji: And how long have you been waiting for that client's answer?

Sofía: For three days. I have been checking my email every five minutes.

Ben: News! The client has just answered!

Sofía: Finally! ...He wants it 'more blue, but less blue' again.

Reading

Abuela Carmen has been living in the same house since 1980, and she has been cooking in the same kitchen for more than forty years. 'People ask me: Abuela, how long have you been making this recipe? Since before your parents met, my dear.' This morning she has been preparing a special lunch since six o'clock, because Kenji's parents have just arrived from Japan. Kenji has been practicing one Spanish sentence for a week: 'Mucho gusto, la comida está deliciosa.' He has just said it perfectly — and Abuela Carmen has been smiling since that moment.

  • Abuela Carmen has been living in the same house since 1980.
  • She has been cooking in that kitchen for ten years.
  • Kenji's parents have just arrived from Japan.
  • Kenji has been practicing his sentence for a month.
