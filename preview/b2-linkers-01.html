Linking Words — Contrast, Addition, Cause, Result & More

B2 — writing, speaking, reading

Kenji: My essays feel choppy — short sentence, short sentence, short sentence.

Sofía: You need linkers. Although and however are your best friends for contrast.

Kenji: And when I want to add another point?

Sofía: Moreover, furthermore, in addition. Pick one and stick with it in the same paragraph.

Common mistake: “The trip was expensive, however, we decided to go.” — Correct: “The trip was expensive. However, we decided to go.”

Linking words connect ideas and show the relationship between them — contrast, addition, cause, result or example — and each one has its own punctuation rules.

Linkers by function
FunctionLinkersExample
Contrastalthough, even though, however, neverthelessAlthough it rained, we went out.
Additionmoreover, furthermore, in additionShe's talented; moreover, she works hard.
Examplefor example, for instance, such asMany jobs, such as design, need creativity.
Causebecause of, due toThe delay was due to the weather.
Resulttherefore, as a result, consequentlyIt rained; as a result, we stayed home.
Conclusionin conclusion, to sum upIn conclusion, the plan worked.
  • contraste although / even though — aunque
  • contraste however / nevertheless — sin embargo
  • adición moreover / furthermore — además
  • ejemplificación for example / such as — por ejemplo / tal como
  • causa because of / due to — debido a / por causa de
  • consecuencia therefore / as a result — por lo tanto / como resultado
  • cierre in conclusion / to sum up — en conclusión / en resumen
  • Although Ben lost his phone again, he stayed calm.
  • Sofía finished the project; moreover, she did it early.
  • There are many phrasal verbs, such as 'get up' and 'look up'.
  • The flight was delayed due to bad weather.
  • Sofía didn't finish on time; however, the client didn't mind.
  • Kenji doesn't understand everything; nevertheless, he keeps trying.
  • The trip wasn't cancelled, even though it rained all day.
  • Why did you leave, even though the party had just started?
  • Didn't they still go out, despite the rain?
  • Can you give an example, such as a specific situation?
  • Was the meeting cancelled because of the storm? Yes, it was.
  • Did they finish, in spite of the delay? Yes, they did.
  • Is there a clear result, therefore? Yes, there is.

Practice

  1. ___ Ben lost his phone again, he stayed calm. (Although, Therefore, For example)
  2. Sofía finished the project; ___, she did it early. (moreover, however, because)
  3. The flight was delayed ___ bad weather. (due to, although, therefore)
  4. 'However' usually starts a new sentence or clause, not the middle of one without punctuation.
  5. 'Such as' introduces an example.
  6. 'Therefore' shows contrast.
  7. Kenji doesn't understand everything; ___, he keeps trying.
  8. There are many phrasal verbs, ___ 'get up' and 'look up'.
  9. It rained all day; ___, they cancelled the trip.
  10. Unscramble: although, it, rained, we, went, out
  11. Unscramble: the, delay, was, due, to, the, weather
  12. Find the mistake: The trip was expensive, however, we decided to go.
  13. It rained. We went out anyway. — Combine using 'although'.
  14. She's talented. She also works hard. — Combine using 'moreover'.
  15. It rained. We stayed home. — Combine using 'as a result'.
  16. Because of the storm, the flight was cancelled. — Write a sentence using 'in spite of' instead (the flight was NOT cancelled).
  17. Give one example of a linker of contrast.

Listening

Kenji: My essays feel choppy — short sentence, short sentence, short sentence.

Sofía: You need linkers. 'Although' and 'however' are your best friends for contrast.

Kenji: And when I want to add another point?

Sofía: 'Moreover', 'furthermore', 'in addition'. Pick one and stick with it in the same paragraph.

Kenji: What about giving an example?

Sofía: 'For example' or 'such as'. Simple as that.

Reading

Sofía has read enough of Kenji's essays to know his one weakness: linkers. His ideas are always good, but they arrive one after another with no connection, like a list instead of an argument. 'Although your grammar is strong,' she told him once, 'your paragraphs don't flow.' She made him a short list: although and however for contrast, moreover and in addition for adding a point, for example and such as when he needs proof, and therefore or as a result when one idea causes another. Kenji printed it and taped it above his desk. Weeks later, his essays still aren't perfect — however, they finally sound like arguments instead of lists.

  • Sofía says Kenji's ideas are usually weak.
  • Sofía gave Kenji a short list of linkers.
  • Kenji ignored the list completely.
  • His essays now sound like arguments instead of lists.
